Learning Intentions & Success Criteria

Learning Intentions
  • Recognise and apply index laws to simplify expressions.
  • Use index notation to represent repeated multiplication.
Success Criteria
  • I can identify which index law to use in a problem.
  • I can simplify expressions such as am · an, am / an, (am)n, (ab)n, a0 (with a ≠ 0).

Idea: Same base? Compare how many copies remain after division.

So for division I subtract the exponents. That makes sense.

And here is the power of a power. A power raised to another power multiplies the indices.

Idea: Repeated groups of a power combine by multiplication of indices.

Helpful thought: Think “groups of groups” — multiply the exponents.

Worked solution: (y2)3 = y2·3 = y6.

Multiply the exponents when a power is raised to a power. Noted.
