Prerequisite Revision — Indices

A friendly warm‑up: vocabulary, index notation, expanded form, and order of operations with powers.

Learning Intentions & Success Criteria

Learning Intentions
  • Recall what indices mean and use index notation accurately.
  • Switch between expanded form and index form with confidence.
  • Evaluate simple powers and apply the correct order of operations.
Success Criteria
  • I can identify the base and the index in an expression like a5.
  • I can convert a × a × a × a to a4 and expand (ab)2 to (ab)(ab).
  • I can evaluate powers such as 25 and use powers in calculations like 32 + 42.

Phase 1 — I do

Listen in and get comfortable with the language of indices.

TTeacher
Index notation is a shortcut for repeated multiplication. In a5, the letter is the base and the small number is the index (or exponent).
In a5, what is the base?
Vocabulary focus: base vs index.

Helpful thought: The base is the repeated factor being multiplied.

Worked solution: Base is a.

In a5, what is the index?
Vocabulary focus: base vs index.

Helpful thought: The index shows how many copies of the base are multiplied.

Worked solution: Index is 5.

SStudent
So the base is the “thing” being repeated and the index is how many times. Clear!
Write a × a × a × a using index notation.
Convert expanded multiplication to index form.

Helpful thought: Count the number of a factors.

Worked solution: a4.

Evaluate: 25
Index notation as repeated multiplication with numbers.

Helpful thought: Multiply five copies of 2 together.

Worked solution: 32.

Phase 2 — We do

Let’s practise together with gentle prompts.

TTeacher
Sometimes we go from powers back to expanded form to see the structure clearly.
Write b3 in expanded multiplication (no exponents).
Show the repeated multiplication explicitly.

Helpful thought: Think three copies of b multiplied.

Worked solution: b × b × b (or b*b*b).

SStudent
Seeing the factors laid out helps me spot patterns.
TTeacher
Brackets tell us which factors belong together before applying an index.
Write (2a)3 in expanded multiplication (no simplification).
Brackets group factors — copy the whole bracket each time.

Helpful thought: Treat (2a) as one “chunk” and repeat it.

Worked solution: (2a)(2a)(2a) or 2a × 2a × 2a.

TTeacher
We also use powers inside calculations. Exponents come before multiplication and addition.
Evaluate: 32 + 42
Order of operations: evaluate powers before adding.

Helpful thought: Work out each power, then add.

Worked solution: 9 + 16 = 25.

In 5a3, what is the base of the power?
Distinguish coefficient (number in front) from the base.

Helpful thought: The base is the part raised to a power.

Worked solution: Base is a (the 5 is the coefficient).
